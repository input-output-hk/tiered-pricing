# [Experimentation](https://github.com/input-output-hk/tiered-pricing/issues/54)

This fortnight broadened the experiment set from the single severe-congestion workload to five load profiles plus two trickle profiles, found and repaired a genuine design flaw at light loads, and stress-tested the recommended mechanism along the parameter and demand-elasticity axes. The design that emerges is `both-dynamic-strict-threshold`: ranking blocks reserved for urgent transactions at all times, with an endorser block announced only when its payload reaches a byte threshold or when an age escape fires. Full details in the [preliminary report](https://github.com/input-output-hk/tiered-pricing/blob/cip-urgency-signalling/docs/phase-2/preliminary-experiment-report.md).

The load profiles added: `low` (3 tx/slot, below RB saturation), `mid-load` (5 tx/slot, just above), `eb-capacity-stress` (peaks to ~396 tx/slot against the EB byte cap), `launch-day` (offered demand pinned at the EB byte capacity with an urgency-skewed mix, calibrated to the January 2022 SundaeSwap launch), and trickle profiles at 0.1 and 0.5 tx/slot.

The main findings, in the order they drove the design:

- **Plain RB reservation regresses below flat fee at low load** (56.77% vs 58.79% urgent retained value). Every scrap of standard overflow triggers a thin EB whose certificate consumes more ranking-block space than the payload it delivers, so urgent transactions lose blocks to certificates.
- **A strict EB announcement threshold repairs the regression.** An EB may only be announced when its payload reaches `max((1 - targetUtilisation) × RB byte cap, RB byte cap / 2)`, which is half the RB cap at the default target. Every certificate is then worth the block space it consumes: +3.03 ± 1.11 percentage points urgent retained value over plain reservation at low load (ten of ten seeds), restoring statistical parity with flat fee in the one regime where reservation used to lose. Both halves of the rule reference only on-chain data, so it stays ledger-enforceable.
- **The threshold alone can starve a trickle**, so we added a time-gated escape: an EB may also be announced once K ranking blocks have passed since the last announcement. At a 0.1 tx/slot trickle the pure threshold starves the standard lane totally (0% of its value retained); the escape at K = 10 repairs it (+83.39 ± 8.59 percentage points standard retained value, ten of ten seeds) at no measurable urgent-lane cost. At ordinary low load K = 10 is bit-identical to no escape in every seed, so the rule is inert outside the regime it exists for.
- **The launch-day profile hardens the both-dynamic preference into a requirement.** Reservation over a statically-priced standard lane delivers nothing under sustained saturation (unpriced standard traffic squats in the shared mempool and starves the reserved lane at admission, leaving priority-only-reserved statistically indistinguishable from flat fee), while both-dynamic under the same reservation rule beats flat fee by +5.83 ± 4.22 percentage points of offered value (eight of ten seeds).
- **A parameter stress test bounds the controller settings.** A sweep of target utilisation {0.25, 0.5, 0.75} × max-change denominator {4, 8, 16}, ten seeds, four loads. Inside the envelope (target 0.5-0.75, denominator 8-16) the mechanism never falls below flat fee at any load; outside it the failures are informative rather than gradual (target 0.25 retains less value than flat fee under launch-day load; denominator 4 is price-unstable at every load). The recommended calibration moved from the denominator-8 anchor to denominator 16: welfare-equivalent at every swept load at baseline elasticity, and it eliminates price shocks.
- **A demand-elasticity stress test** (all values scaled 10×; 10-25% of arrivals at 100× values; each mix paired with its own flat-fee control) preserves the advantage at every mix and shows it growing with the share of high-value demand: the launch-day paired delta rises from +84G lovelace at the base mix to +8,624G at the harshest. Denominator 16 remains shock-free at every elasticity tested.
- **A cross-lane multiplier floor was tested and rejected** (variants at 3× and 16×): it overprices the urgent lane precisely when capacity is slack, costing 9-15 percentage points of urgent retained value at low load.

The severe-congestion headline is unchanged: the recommended mechanism improves urgent retained value from 44.32% (flat fee) to 51.55% (+7.23 ± 1.64 percentage points, paired over ten seeds) and reduces urgent mean latency from 2.91 to 2.44 blocks, and it now never falls below the flat-fee baseline at any load or elasticity tested inside the validated parameter envelope.

Simulator work supporting this: per-experiment load profile selection; the new load and trickle profiles; the strict-threshold reservation policy (replacing the earlier conditional reservation) and the `ebAgeEscapeRbIntervals` age-escape policy field; sweep manifests for parameter robustness, demand elasticity, the multiplier floor, and trickle aging; removal of the retired `sim-rs` simulator.
