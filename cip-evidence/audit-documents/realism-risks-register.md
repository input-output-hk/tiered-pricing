**Disclosure-paragraph:** This Cardano Improvement Proposal (CIP) uses four pricing-controller calibration values; one is anchored against the Ethereum Improvement Proposal 1559 (EIP-1559) academic-critique tradition and three carry "conditional on X" disclosures per the anchor-or-disclose work in `.planning/phases/04-refresh-and-anchor/04-01-DOC-03-anchor-search.md`.

(a) *Window length 32 priced blocks for capacity-varying signals* (single-lane Ethereum Improvement Proposal 1559 (EIP-1559); both-dynamic standard lane; un-reserved priority lane). The choice of a smoothing window over Ethereum's unwindowed per-block update is motivated by the published Ethereum Improvement Proposal 1559 (EIP-1559) academic-critique tradition: Reijsbergen et al. Advances in Financial Technologies (AFT) 2021 documents chaotic short-term oscillations in block sizes and slow adjustments during demand bursts under the unwindowed deployed controller; Leonardos et al. AFT 2021 gives a complementary theoretical bounded-oscillation result; Liu et al. Conference on Computer and Communications Security (CCS) 2022 reports the empirical counter-bound that this instability has not yet manifested as a usability problem on Ethereum mainnet. Phase-2 picks a capacity-weighted window over the literature's preferred additive-increase / multiplicative-decrease (AIMD) response because the linear-Leios block-mix (ranking blocks (RBs) at ~90 kilobytes (KiB) versus endorser blocks (EBs) up to 12 megabytes (MiB); ratio ≈ 133×) requires capacity-weighting that AIMD does not provide. The specific length 32 is a round-number choice within the smoothing band; the `phase-2-eip1559-smoothing` suite sweeps {16, 32, 64} for sensitivity. Welfare findings are reported under window length 32; the unwindowed Ethereum-equivalent (window = 1) and over-smoothed (window = 128) regimes were not exercised, but the {16, 32, 64} sweep brackets the operating value within a factor of two.

(b) *Multiplier floor 4* in `phase-2-rb-scarcity` and `phase-2-urgency-inversion`. The choice is an internal calibration accommodation, not an externally anchored economic claim: no deployed dynamic-pricing system has a second-lane multiplier floor at all, so the Ethereum Improvement Proposal 1559 (EIP-1559) academic-critique literature does not motivate a specific magnitude. The value 4 is chosen so that urgency ≥ 2 actor components self-select into the priority lane and surface controller drift in two specific suites; at the spec default 16, priority demand stays too thin to surface drift in these two suites (per `CLAUDE.md` §"Calibration choices"). The companion test TEST-07a (`../test-results/multiplier-floor-16-companion/results.md`) confirms that both suites' welfare findings are regime-dependent at floor = 16 — the ranking-block-reserved (RB-reserved) scarcity finding inverts and the urgency-inversion finding weakly reverses. Welfare findings from `phase-2-rb-scarcity` and `phase-2-urgency-inversion` are reported under multiplier-floor 4; the remaining five goldens-pinned suites cover the spec default 16, and the two priority-only suites (`phase-2-priority-only-rb-reserved` and `phase-2-priority-only-unreserved`) further sweep {4, 8, 16} for cross-floor sensitivity. Welfare findings are conditional on multiplier-floor 4; the regime-dependence at floor 16 is itself disclosed.

(c) *Multiplier floor 16* (the spec default). The choice has no external anchor: no deployed dynamic-pricing system has a second-lane multiplier, and the Ethereum Improvement Proposal 1559 (EIP-1559) academic-critique literature addresses single-lane controllers only.
