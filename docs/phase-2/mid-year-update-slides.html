Before

Today: every transaction waits in the same line

  • One flat fee. Fees do not respond to congestion, and there is no way to pay to be included sooner.
  • No way for a transaction to say it is time-sensitive, so nodes cannot serve urgent intents first.
  • Linear-Leios adds a second, high-capacity inclusion path: two block types with different wait profiles, and no way for a transaction to choose between them.

After

Urgency signalling: a fast lane the ledger enforces

Urgent → pays a dynamic quote → eligible for Ranking Blocks: fastest inclusion
Standard → pays its own dynamic quote → included via Endorser Blocks

Ledger-enforced rule: a Ranking Block may only contain transactions paying the urgent quote, so block producers cannot sell fast-lane access below the posted price. Both lanes are dynamic by design (EIP-1559 style). At quiet times the standard quote rests at today's minimum fee, and an Endorser Block is announced only once its payload is worth its certificate: at least half a Ranking Block's capacity.

Slide 2 · Before · about 2.5 minutes

  • The problem. Flat fee, one queue. No way to say "time-sensitive": liquidations, oracle updates, arbitrage windows.
  • Leios makes it timely. Big throughput win, delivered through two block types: RBs frequent and immediate, EBs carrying the bulk and settling later. The wait now depends on the path, and transactions cannot pick their path. If asked: Leios is not slower for the same load; the point is choice, not slowdown.
  • CPS-0031. We wrote the problem statement up formally, as part of this project.
  • Phase 1 = theory. Analysed "Tiered Mechanisms for Blockchain Transaction Fees". Cardano diverges: two block types, variable tx sizes, two-dimensional pricing. Direct port wrong shape.
  • Feedback shaped this once already. Full tiered pricing trimmed to a simple urgency signal after community discussion, and Carlos Lopez De Lara was at Buidlerfest talking with community members about this work. Today is round two.
  • The number. 44.32% of urgent value retained (flat fee, severe congestion). Retained value = value-weighted share of urgent demand served in time; the rest lost to delay or failure.

Slide 3 · After · about 5.5 minutes

  • Mechanism · ~1.5 min
    • Two lanes, chosen by the transaction. Urgent: dynamic quote, eligible for RBs. Standard: own quote, via EBs.
    • Quotes adjust per lane after every block, EIP-1559 style.
    • Ledger-enforced: RBs may only contain urgent-quote payers. Closes bribery: no private deal can sell fast-lane access below the posted price.
    • Own it: both lanes are dynamic, deliberately. We know a dynamic standard lane is debated; we think it is the best way forward. Evidence: at launch-day load a statically priced standard lane delivers nothing (it cannot shed demand, jams the shared mempool, and starves the reserved lane). Reassurance: at quiet times the standard quote rests at today's minimum fee, and an EB is only announced once its payload reaches half an RB's capacity, so standard-lane dynamism only engages under real load.
  • Validation · ~2.5 min
    • Experiments (Will): ten seeded runs per mechanism, multiple loads incl. launch-day calibrated to SundaeSwap, Jan 2022.
    • 44.32% → 50.97% urgent retained value (+6.65 ± 2.40 pp, ~15% relative). Latency 2.91 → 2.52 blocks.
    • Walk the chart: beats flat fee at every contended load (+3.04 mid, +6.65 severe, +5.83 launch-day, +7.38 EB-stress); parity at low load (+1.01, CI spans zero, by design). Latency 0.06 to 0.81 blocks sooner; launch-day gains via admission instead.
    • Stress tests: holds across a validated parameter envelope, not fragile.
    • Prototype (Nicolas): ledger rules + fee controller + two-lane mempool on the real node stack, end to end on a three-node private devnet. Both lanes forge and clear. Early, happy path, but real code on a real network.
    • Formal spec (Polina): transaction commutativity proof under way in the formal ledger spec; underpins the reordering the two-lane design relies on.
    • Incentives (Giorgos): analysis in the next 6 months. Deferred until the CIP is finalised, so it analyses what the community converges on.
  • Where we are: CIP nearly ready. Theory, simulation, and the devnet prototype are behind us; the formal spec proof and the CIP draft are the pieces still moving (the draft has open sections). What's left is the community's part: feedback now, CIP finalised end of July, incentive analysis in the 6 months after.
